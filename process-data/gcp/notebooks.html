Notebooks

Jupyter Notebooks are supported on GCP, you can locate these under AI Platform (doc), alongside a variety of other tools. You can find the official documentation for AI Platform Notebooks at cloud.google.com.

Below we have created a step-by-step guide for starting with Notebooks on GCP.

Guide#

  1. Go to AI Platform/Notebooks
  2. Choose New Instance and Customize Instance
  3. Enter all the mandatory fields:
    • Instance name
    • Region - Choose one of the European
    • Zone - A zone within the region you chose
    • Environment - Choose a suitable environment based on your needs/programming language
    • Machine type and GPU - Resource requirements
  4. Press Create
  5. When the machine is up and running press Open JupyterLab

Machine type and GPU#

You know best what you need, therefore there are no limits for what you can choose. However, remember that this could become quite expensive if you keep your machine running for a long time without it being used.

Stop a machine#

If you have a machine that you do not need at the moment, but you do not want to delete it, you can stop it temporarily instead. Choose the machine here and press STOP.

Delete a machine#

If you have a machine you do not need anymore, you can delete it. Choose the machine here and press DELETE.
